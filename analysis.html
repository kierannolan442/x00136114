This graph shows the total vaccine count and how that is broken down per continent. The larger the chart, the more vaccines there are! See the progression we've made with combatting the pandemic.






















Different countries handled the pandemic differently. With this graph, I wanted to show if those different approaches to the pandemic in terms of lockdowns and restrictions had an effect on the deaths per million people. Click a country and find out how strictly they managed the pandemic. Countries on the left had a much stricter approach to handling the pandemic. Decide for yourself if more stringent measures led to an overall lower death count. I personally found that stricter regulations had a lower death count with some exceptions such as Taiwan and New Zealand.

This dashboard aims to group the countries in a way. By clicking a country from the bar chart, it will first show the deaths per million experienced by country. It will also highlight in the left side graph where that country ranked in terms of percentage of death and total cases. This dashboard's overall goal is to show which countries experienced the worst of the pandemic, shown on the outside, and which did better, closer to the inside.






















Life expectency varies widely from country to country. This graph shows how the life expectency of a country relates to the deaths of the country. The orange dot shows life expectency and the red dot shows deaths per million.
